Crossbow

This project won best design and knocked over the block tower successfully.

A crossbow that I and three other team members designed in Autodesk Inventor. Pieces were then laser cut and assembled using MDF or Medium Density Fiberboard. What's interesting about it is that it was required to knock over a small tower, and out of the entire class, ours was one of the very few (and most accurate) ones to do so. Also all pieces are cut out flat, so we had to create a vision to bring 2D into 3D. In addition, it launches glue-stick sized projectiles, but includes a 3D printed one with our initials.

  • Date: Early 2015 · Documentation

Dublin High School Gael Force Robotics Club

Gael Force Robotics is our school's robotics club. I was project lead for web development on the front end, Python back end, team performance database, and blog. I also served as a System Administrator for our server, working with the Linux Terminal and Nginx. I also worked formerly on Node.js. This front end was made in a two month time frame with fellow developers Devin Mui and Billy Zhong.

Laser Harp

Lasers + Arduino = music

The Laser Harp is a simple Arduino based piece of hardware with the ability to shoot lasers and create music using the laser "strings". It was created using Arduino C, knowledge of circuits, and knowledge of microcontrollers. This project was originally created to be a laser ukulele, and we purchased a ukulele and disassembled it. However, as the project progressed we found that a harp would be a more feasible project to complete within the time constraints. We used a picture frame as the backbone of the harp design and drilled holes to place the lasers and sensors into.

3D Modeled Toy Train

This engineering assignment was to 3D model a stylish toy train car and engine to fit on a track of specified dimensions. Here, I choose the design, selected the colors, and modeled all the necessary parts in Autodesk Inventor. Next, I assembled the pieces into a toy train and constrained the movement so that only the wheels turn when animated. The next part of the assignment was to join the train car with other students' designs to ensure that all the designs were interchangeable.
