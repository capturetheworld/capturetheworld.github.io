About ME

I'm Ian SooHoo. I like technology, engineering, and design. I currently attend Dublin High School as a Junior. As someone who loves to understand how things work and someone who uses technology in daily, I am interested in an opportunity to work in a career that lets me create devices and services to help enrich others' lives. I love to stay ahead of the curve and to learn new concepts such as AI and Big Data. Though I specialize in Software Development, Website Development, and Software Design, I try to stay up to date on the latest in other areas.

I am always excited to learn new things and to help teach others as well. I believe working in a team helps bring fresh ideas to the table that I alone could not have thought of. Since everyone has different backgrounds and experiences, coming to a solution together in a team will be quicker and better. I believe that I am a calm and creative leader and that I have had a head start in experience. Currently I have experience in Autodesk Inventor CAD, video editing, Java, HTML, CSS, Javascript, and on the server side, DNS, some Sysadmin experience (from Robotics), and Nginx. In terms of video editing, I have edited 3 videos for school projects in iMovie and Premiere Pro, as well and have worked with green screens.

My goal is to earn a degree in Computer Science and to use that degree to find a career in Software Design and User Experience. I have always had a passion and will continue to have a fire burn inside of me to adapt and grow as a person, not only in knowledge but also understanding. One of my hobbies is video editing, and another is voluenteering. I have edited family movies in my spare time, also working with Photoshop and Illustrator. I voluenteer at church as a young elementary leader and on the Production Team as a voluenteer ensuring that the computer graphics function properly.

Crossbow

A laser cut crossbow completely designed from scratch.

A crossbow that I and three other team members designed in Autodesk Inventor. Pieces were then laser cut and assembled using MDF or Medium Density Fiberboard. What's interesting about it is that it was required to knock over a small tower, and out of the entire class, ours was one of the very few (and most accurate) ones to do so. Also all pieces are cut out flat, so we had to create a vision to bring 2D into 3D. In addition, it launches glue-stick sized projectiles, but includes a 3D printed one with our initials.

  • Date: Early 2015

The website

Gael Force Robotics is our school's robotics club. I was project lead for web development on the front end, Python back end, team performance database, and blog. Laser Harp

Arduinos, lasers, music

The Laser Harp is a simple Arduino based piece of hardware with the ability to shoot lasers and create music using the laser "strings". It was created using Arduino C, knowledge of circuits, and knowledge of microcontrollers.
