About ME

I'm Ian SooHoo and I am a student developer and software designer currently attending San Jose State University and majoring in Computer Science. I have a passion for technology, computers, and, specifically, design. As someone who loves to understand how things work, and someone who uses technology throughout their day, I am interested in an opportunity to work in a career that lets me create devices and services to help enrich others' lives. I love to stay ahead of the curve, and to learn about new concepts such as AI and Big Data. Though I specialize in Software development and design, I try to stay up to date on the latest in other areas. One such area includes web development.

I am always excited to learn new things and to help teach other. A fundamental belief of mine is that working in a team helps bring fresh ideas to the table that I alone could not have thought of. Since everyone has different backgrounds and experiences, there will be better solutions to a problem. As a calm and creative leader, I love to collaborate on coding and development. Currently I have experience in Autodesk Inventor CAD, video editing, Java, HTML, CSS, JavaScript; On the server side, DNS, some Sysadmin experience (from Robotics), and Nginx. One hobby of mine, video editing, has become more serious in recent years. Some of my works can be viewed on the Video Production page above. Most of my original work was done in iMovie and Final Cut, but recently I have begun using Premiere Pro and After Effects.

My goal is to earn a degree in Computer Science, and to use that degree to find a career in Software Development and Design. I have always had a passion, and will continue to have a fire burn inside of me to adapt and grow as a person, not only in knowledge but also understanding. One of my hobbies is design editing, and another is volunteering. I have created logos my spare time utilizing Photoshop and Illustrator. I volunteer at church as a kids’ leader and on the Production Team as a Technical Manager operating both technical direction and computer graphics.

Crossbow

This project won best design and knocked over the block tower successfully.

A crossbow that I and three other team members designed in Autodesk Inventor. Pieces were then laser cut and assembled using MDF or Medium Density Fiberboard. What's interesting about it is that it was required to knock over a small tower, and out of the entire class, ours was one of the very few (and most accurate) ones to do so. Also all pieces are cut out flat, so we had to create a vision to bring 2D into 3D. In addition, it launches glue-stick sized projectiles, but includes a 3D printed one with our initials.

Laser Harp

Lasers + Arduino = music

The Laser Harp is a simple Arduino based piece of hardware with the ability to shoot lasers and create music using the laser "strings". It was created using Arduino C, knowledge of circuits, and knowledge of microcontrollers. This project was originally created to be a laser ukulele, and we purchased a ukulele and disassembled it. However, as the project progressed we found that a harp would be a more feasible project to complete within the time constraints. We used a picture frame as the backbone of the harp design and drilled holes to place the lasers and sensors into.

3D Modeled Toy Train

This engineering assignment was to 3D model a stylish toy train car and engine to fit on a track of specified dimensions. Here, I choose the design, selected the colors, and modeled all the necessary parts in Autodesk Inventor. Next, I assembled the pieces into a toy train and constrained the movement so that only the wheels turn when animated. The next part of the assignment was to join the train car with other students' designs to ensure that all the designs were interchangeable.

Recycled Racer

A Recycle Racer "Mousetrap Car"

r challenge was to create a car powered by either a mouse trap our some other form of power without the need of human interaction to keep the car moving. We designed the mouse-trap powered racecar from common recycled household items. This project took approximately 3 weeks to complete, and we were limited in the materials we were allowed to use. For example, the wheels are made from discs, the body from tongue depressors, and the tension wire from ribbon.

One of the early challenges was that our initial design failed the first distance test. However, as a team we pulled together to redesign the power-drive mechanism which in turn delivered enough power to keep the car going, exceeding the required distance during the second test.

AP Physics Indicator

The breadboard with integrated circuits and an output LED.

The purpose of my project was to utilize create and breadboard a circuit from scratch using simplified AOI logic with multiple inputs and an output, and to utilize AND and OR gates to produce the desired output.

Selecting the most difficult AP class on campus was a challenging choice for me. I decided to use four input switches, 2 based on grade inputs, and 2 on subjective inputs. Then, I created conditions for when my LED should be on and generated both a truth table and Boolean logic equation from those conditions. After simulating the circuit in Multisim (file can be downloaded below), I breadboarded the circuit on a TI MyDAQ. I then calculated the estimated cost for building a circuit and completed the project within two weeks. I believe that additional inputs should be implemented in the future, to extend the circuit's accuracy: e.g. inputs Science class grades and other math class grades.
